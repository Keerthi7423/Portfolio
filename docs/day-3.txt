2. TECHNICAL DECISIONS & RATIONALE (Interview Preparation)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Why define spacing and radius as CSS variables rather than just using Tailwind's default theme?
A: Design consistency and flexibility. By defining our own tokens (e.g., --space-10), we enforce a strict design language. It also makes the system framework-agnostic—if we ever move away from Tailwind or use a component library that doesn't support it, the core design tokens remain accessible via standard CSS.

Q: Explain the choice of 'ease-out-expo' for section reveals.
A: Cinematic feel. `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` provides a very fast initial movement that slows down gracefully. This mimics "momentum"—it feels like an heavy object (like a Stark panel) being projected and then settling into place with high precision.

Q: What is the purpose of the 'scanline' animation?
A: It adds "micro-life" to the UI. Static designs feel dead. By adding a very subtle, slow-moving scanline animation (opacity 0.03), we simulate the behavior of a high-tech monitor. It keeps the user's brain engaged without being a distraction.

Q: How does 'lerp' (Linear Interpolation) relate to your duration standards?
A: While we haven't implemented the custom cursor yet, our duration standards like `--duration-instant` (80ms) are designed to work alongside lerp factors. 80ms is roughly the "perceived instant" limit; anything slower feels laggy, anything faster feels jarring. Our standards ensure that every interaction feels engineered, not accidental.
