================================================================================
  DAY 4: MARVEL-STYLE LOADING SCREEN & SYSTEM INITIALIZATION
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
Today's mission was to implement a high-end, cinematic loading experience that
sets the tone for the entire portfolio. Inspired by the Marvel Studios intro,
the Loading Screen handles asset pre-sequencing and provides a narrative bridge 
into the 3D world.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE: THE LOADING FLOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- STATE MANAGEMENT: Used React's `useState` and `useEffect` in the root `page.js` 
  to orchestrate the transition. The `isLoading` state controls whether the 
  `LoadingScreen` or the main content is rendered.
  
- TRANSITION ENGINE: Leveraged `AnimatePresence` from Framer Motion. This allows 
  for "exit animations"—meaning the loader doesn't just disappear; it scales and 
  fades out smoothly while the next section fades in.

- DURATION: Synchronized to ~3.5 seconds to balance "wow factor" with user 
  patience (UX balance).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. COMPONENT DEEP DIVE: LoadingScreen.jsx
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. LETTER-BY-LETTER ASSEMBLY (GSAP):
   - Logic: The name "STARK INDUSTRIES" (or developer name) is split into 
     individual <span> elements.
   - Animation: GSAP's `.to()` method with a `stagger` of 0.05s.
   - Cinematic Flare: Added a `filter: blur()` and `y: 20` offset on the initial 
     state, which resolves to crisp, aligned text. This mimics the "focusING" 
     effect of a high-tech HUD.

B. METALLIC SHIMMER EFFECT:
   - Implementation: Created a linear-gradient background on the text with 
     `background-size: 200%`.
   - Motion: GSAP constantly animates the `background-position` from 0% to 200%, 
     creating a moving highlight that looks like light reflecting off metal.

C. ENERGY CHARGE PROGRESS BAR:
   - Logic: A mock progress state increments randomly every 100ms to simulate 
     dynamic loading.
   - Visuals: A 2px high bar with a `marvel-red` background and a 
     `hologram-glow-red` shadow (defined in globals.css).
   - Interaction: A "Skip Sequence" button appears after 1.5s (GSAP/Framer Motion 
     stagger) to ensure accessibility for repeat visitors.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. AESTHETICS & DESIGN TOKENS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- SCANLINES: Applied the `.scanlines` utility class, which uses a CSS linear 
  gradient and a infinite keyframe animation to simulate an old CRT or 
  high-tech monitor.
  
- NOISE OVERLAY: Applied `.noise-bg` which layers a subtle grain (0.05 opacity) 
  over the entire screen, making the digital colors feel more "physical" 
  and cinematic.

- COLOR PALETTE: 
    - Secondary BG: --color-void (#040408)
    - Accents: --color-marvel-red (#e23636) and --color-stark-gold (#c8a951)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. INTERVIEW PREPARATION: "HOW WOULD YOU EXPLAIN THIS?"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Why use GSAP and Framer Motion together?
A: GSAP is the industry standard for complex, high-performance timeline 
   animations (like the letter-by-letter stagger with blur). Framer Motion is 
   superior for React-native state transitions, such as mounting/unmounting 
   components and handling "exit" animations that are hard to do in raw CSS.

Q: How do you optimize a loading screen for performance?
A: We use the loading period to preload critical assets (like fonts or 3D 
   models). In this build, I've used standard React lifecycle methods to ensure 
   the heavy Hero section code doesn't start executing until the user is 
   primed by the loader.

Q: What is the significance of the "Skip" button?
A: User Experience (UX). While a cinematic intro is great for a first 
   impression, forcing a user to watch it every time they visit can lead to 
   high bounce rates. Providing a skip option after a slight delay (1.5s) 
   respects the user's time while still delivering the brand impact.
